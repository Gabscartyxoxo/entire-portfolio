Phase 4 Course Goals



1. Foster the creative habits of mind

This phase of the project was kind of the end all be all of this class. That being said, it was a demonstration of all of the skills that we had learned in our class so far and puting them together. For my project I picked a topic that I both enjoy and know well, black people (espcially) women in fashion. I especially enjoyed learning and researching different sectors of fashion and history and being able to put it together in a webpage interactive experience.

2. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

Though I wasn't there for the final showing of projects, reflecting on the classes leading up to the showcase, it was really interesting to see other people's projects. Overall, that was the best part of the class in general. I'm usually a person who hates presenting and can be rather shy in presenting my material. I really liked sitting in class and listening to peoples passions, ideas, interests, and thoughts. I think a lot of my classmates had really cool things that they worked on.

3. Think deeply about craft.

As I explained in the beginning, I picked a topic that I know on a personal level and that I find interesting enough to do research on. The way in which I presented the project took the most thinking. I really wanted to get better at understanding html and website building, and I can say that I've improved since the first time earlier this semester. I learned to add videos and photos. I learned to change text font, text color. I learned to make images bigger upon hovering with the mouse. I learned to link one website to other websites that I built. I learned some background affects. I learned about code organization. There's a ton of other things that I could include, but most of all I learned patience with code. Coding can be frustrating and it can take a while, but luckily I was able to carve out time to research and use my resources so I didn't get completely lost.

4. Engage in sustained research and skills development.

This project took a lot of time and research. I learned a lot about fashion as an art form and way to express culture. And as stated previously, A lot of research went into the coding aspect of this project. I wish I had the time and energy to do more, but I do have some ideas for future projects.

5. Learn to present your work

As discussed before I really valued the time to share projects and classwork. I'm still not the kind of person who enjoys presenting in front of the class, but I've gotten generally better at it and I enjoy hearing feedback as well.
